About Me

Hello, My name is Chris. Welcome to my very first website! Things may look pretty rudimentary, but I ask that you bare with me on my first adventure into web development.

I am currently a student in Oregon State University's post-baccalaureate computer science program. I have always been a computer enthusiast, but never tried anything more complex than building a tower by myself. When I was exposed to some simple elements of programming through my work in the sciences, I knew right away that it needed to be the next step I take in life. With the advent of COVID-19 I saw an opportunity and applied to OSU for the fall term of 2020. Since then I have been working through the courses while also working as a childcare professional for children with special needs.

Before all of this, I was an analytical chemist working in food chemistry. I had graduated with a degree in biology from University of Wisconsin Oshkosh in 2017 and joined the "labrat" workforce almost right away. The work was engaging and challenging, but I was always looking for something a little extra. One day, I was given the opportunity to work as a superuser for our LIMS (Laboratory Information Management System) software. The training took a month or so, but I was eventually helping my team in an IT capacity. I found this workload to be much more enjoyable than routine lab work so I tried to get as much as possible.

Beyond my experience in the lab, I also have a history in healthcare as a CNA and EMT. I have used this experience to join first responder teams both inside and out of work. I have also leveraged it most recently to start working as a childcare specialist to fill a need gap created by the pandemic.
